# Warren Buffett — Value Investing Framework

## Core Philosophy
Buy wonderful companies at fair prices, hold forever. The stock market is a voting machine
in the short run and a weighing machine in the long run. Time in market beats timing the market.

## Key Principles
1. **Circle of competence.** Only invest in businesses you deeply understand.
2. **Economic moat.** Look for durable competitive advantages (brand, network effects, cost advantages).
3. **Margin of safety.** Only buy at a significant discount to intrinsic value.
4. **Long-term orientation.** "Our favorite holding period is forever." Ignore short-term noise.
5. **Business quality over price.** "It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price
   than a fair company at a wonderful price."
6. **Management matters.** Back honest, capable managers who allocate capital well.
7. **Avoid complexity.** If you don't understand how a business makes money, don't buy it.

## Questions to Ask About Any Portfolio
- Do you understand every business/asset you own?
- Are you holding because of conviction, or fear of missing out?
- Would you be comfortable holding each position for 10 years?
- Are your best ideas getting enough weight, or are you over-diversifying?
- Are you reacting to market noise, or business fundamentals?

## Buffett's View on Common Behaviors
- **Crypto:** Buffett avoids assets that produce no cash flows and are valued purely on sentiment.
- **Trading frequently:** "The stock market is a device for transferring money from the impatient to the patient."
- **Panic selling:** "Be fearful when others are greedy, and greedy when others are fearful."
- **Over-diversification:** "Diversification is protection against ignorance." Concentrated bets on
  your highest-conviction ideas outperform a diluted index.

## Red Flags
- Buying based on price momentum rather than value
- Investing in businesses you cannot explain simply
- Selling quality companies during temporary market downturns
- Chasing hot sectors without understanding the underlying economics
