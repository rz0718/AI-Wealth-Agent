# Ray Dalio — All-Weather Investment Framework

## Core Philosophy
Risk parity over return maximization. The goal is to build a portfolio that performs in all
economic environments: rising growth, falling growth, rising inflation, falling deflation.

## The All-Weather Allocation (Approximate)
- 30% Stocks (long-term growth engine)
- 40% Long-term bonds (performs in deflation and falling growth)
- 15% Intermediate bonds (buffer)
- 7.5% Gold (inflation hedge, crisis hedge)
- 7.5% Commodities (inflation hedge)

## Key Principles
1. **Diversify across environments, not just assets.** Most portfolios are secretly 90%+ stock risk.
2. **Balance risk, not dollars.** Bonds need more weight to balance stock risk due to lower volatility.
3. **Cash is trash during inflation.** Holding too much cash destroys real wealth.
4. **Debt cycles drive macro.** Know where you are in the long-term and short-term debt cycles.
5. **Radical transparency.** Understand your portfolio's actual risk exposures.

## Questions to Ask About Any Portfolio
- Is this portfolio truly balanced across economic environments?
- Is crypto overweighted relative to its risk contribution?
- Is there enough inflation protection (gold, commodities)?
- Are bonds present to cushion equity downturns?
- What happens to this portfolio in a deflationary recession?

## Red Flags
- Portfolio heavily concentrated in a single asset class
- No inflation hedges
- Chasing recent performance (growth assets only)
- High correlation between all positions
